Rengstorff Grade Separation: Project Timeline

Published: 2026-03-07  |  Source: Mountain View Legistar & public records

Project summary: The Rengstorff Avenue Grade Separation (Project 22-27) dates to a February 2014 City Council study session that examined multimodal concepts for eliminating the at-grade Caltrain crossing at Rengstorff Avenue (then designated Project 09-28). A Memorandum of Understanding for preliminary engineering and environmental clearance was executed in November 2018, and the project was formally re-numbered 22-27 and received cooperative agreement funding in November 2022. The professional services agreement for the design and right-of-way phase was approved in June 2024, with construction originally anticipated to follow environmental clearance and design by the late 2020s. By mid-2025, cost estimates exceeded the project budget, prompting City Council to approve cost-reduction measures in November 2025. As of early 2026, the project remains in the design and right-of-way phase.

About this project

The Rengstorff Avenue Grade Separation (Project 22-27) will eliminate the at-grade Caltrain crossing at Rengstorff Avenue, creating either an underpass or overpass. A separate project, the Mountain View Transit Center Grade Separation (Project 21-35), addresses the Castro Street / Evelyn Avenue crossing near the Mountain View Caltrain station and is not covered here.
