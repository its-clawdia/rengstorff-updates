Rengstorff Grade Separation: Project Timeline

Published: 2026-03-07  |  Source: Mountain View Legistar & public records (staff reports)

Project summary: The Rengstorff Avenue Grade Separation (Project 22-27) will depress the intersection of Rengstorff Avenue and Central Expressway below grade, leaving Caltrain tracks at their current elevation. Planning began in 2004; the City Council formally studied multimodal concepts in February 2014 and has supported the underpass design ever since. A preliminary engineering MOU with Caltrain was executed in 2019 (up to $3.5M, City-funded). In November 2022, the project advanced to final design with a Cooperative Agreement that secured $42M in VTA Measure B funds. By January 2024, total project cost was estimated at $325M with a $31M funding gap. By October 2024, the CMGC (Construction Manager/General Contractor) revised the construction estimate sharply upward — total cost rose to $453M and the funding gap ballooned to $159M. After a value engineering effort, the Council approved $58.1M in cost-reduction measures in November 2025, reducing the estimate to ~$395M with a remaining gap of ~$99M. As of early 2026, the project is at 65% design; no construction start date has been set.

Chronological record of council actions specifically relating to the Rengstorff Avenue Grade Separation project. Items about the separate Mountain View Transit Center (Castro Street) Grade Separation project are excluded.

  • February 11, 2014 ↗
    City Council Study Session: Rengstorff Avenue Grade Separation Study, Multimodal Concepts (Project 09-28).
    Council received and reviewed a 2014 report updating the original 2004 feasibility study. The session focused on pedestrian and bicycle access, transitions to adjacent land uses (residential, commercial, Rengstorff Park), and multimodal design options. Council re-affirmed support for the underpass concept — depressing Rengstorff Avenue and Central Expressway below grade while keeping the Caltrain tracks at grade — and directed staff to incorporate bicycle/pedestrian enhancements including an overcrossing to maintain connectivity across Rengstorff Avenue.
    Legistar #14-75
  • November 27, 2018 ↗
    City Council: MOU for Preliminary Engineering and Environmental Clearance Phase (Project 17-37).
    Council authorized an MOU with Caltrain (JPB) for the preliminary engineering and environmental clearance phase, with the City committing up to $3.5M and appropriating an initial $350,000. Caltrain was designated lead agency for all engineering and environmental studies; the City as project sponsor would fund the work and handle public outreach. Council also formally directed staff to notify Caltrain of the City's preferred design: depress Rengstorff/Central Expressway below grade, leave tracks at existing grade. The MOU was executed June 10, 2019, with a target completion of June 2020.
    Legistar #18579
  • November 1, 2022 ↗
    City Council (Consent): Authorize Cooperative Agreement and Appropriate Funding — Project 22-27.
    Council authorized a Cooperative Agreement with Caltrain and VTA for the Final Design, Environmental Permitting, Right-of-Way Engineering, and Right-of-Way Acquisition phases. VTA's board had approved $42M in Measure B Grade Separation funds for the project. The City also appropriated $5M from the Shoreline Regional Park Community Fund (acting as its Board of Directors). The project obtained NEPA categorical exemption and CEQA statutory exemption. The project was formally redesignated Project 22-27.
    Legistar #202344
  • June 27, 2023 ↗
    City Council (Consent): New MOU for Preliminary Engineering and Environmental Clearance Phase — up to $3.5M.
    The 2019 MOU expired December 10, 2022, but Caltrain completed the preliminary engineering/environmental clearance in 2023 — over a year late due to JPB staffing challenges and COVID-19. The City had paid $2.9M of the $3.5M cap under the original MOU; a new MOU (terminating June 30, 2024) was needed solely to process the outstanding final invoices. No new scope was added. With this phase closed out, the final design and right-of-way acquisition phase, authorized by the November 2022 Cooperative Agreement, could formally begin.
    Legistar #203271
  • January 23, 2024 ↗
    City Council Study Session: Caltrain Grade Separation Projects — cost estimates, priorities, and funding direction.
    Staff presented updated cost estimates showing the Rengstorff project at $325M total (including $242M construction, escalated from 2023 market conditions at 35% design), with ~$217M in secured/planned funding and a $108M shortfall. Council was also shown that the Castro Street project had an $82M shortfall. Facing a combined gap neither project could bridge simultaneously, Council directed staff to prioritize Rengstorff and move Castro to interim improvements only. Unexpended Castro Measure B funds were reallocated to Rengstorff, reducing Rengstorff's gap to $31M. In December 2023, AECOM was awarded the final design contract by the JPB.
    Legistar #203571
  • June 25, 2024 ↗
    City Council (Consent): Professional Services Agreement for right-of-way acquisition services — up to $590,000.
    Council approved a PSA with Associated Right of Way Services, Inc. (not-to-exceed $540,000 new work, $590,000 total) for property acquisition, relocation assistance, and appraisal services. The underpass design requires full or partial acquisition of properties affected by the significant grade change, as well as temporary construction easements for staging. Approximately 10% of total project costs are funded by the Shoreline Regional Park Community Fund. Final design by AECOM and selection of the Construction Manager/General Contractor (CMGC) were to commence in summer 2024.
    Legistar #204027
  • October 29, 2024 ↗
    Council Transportation Committee: Grade Separation Projects status update.
    Staff reported that in October 2024, at the 35% design submittal stage, the CMGC (Atkinson and Clark) and a third-party cost estimator revised the construction cost estimate upward from $242M to a range of $280M–$312M. Coupled with increases in other phases, the total project cost rose to $453M and the funding gap jumped from $31M to as high as $159M. The Caltrain design team had significantly underestimated construction costs and contingency; the CMGC brought contractor-level estimating that exposed those gaps. Staff initiated value engineering efforts with Caltrain and VTA.
    Legistar #204678
  • March 4, 2025 ↗
    Council Transportation Committee: Grade Separation Projects status update.
    Committee received a progress report on value engineering efforts underway since late 2024 to address the $159M funding gap. Staff continued coordinating with Caltrain and VTA on cost-reduction options. In August 2025, City Managers from nine regional cities with grade separation projects met with Caltrain leadership to collectively raise concerns about cost overruns, project management, and lack of delivery progress — initiating a quarterly regional collaboration process. (Note: no separate staff report was attached to this Legistar item; details drawn from the September 2025 CTC memo.)
    Legistar #204729
  • September 2, 2025 ↗
    Council Transportation Committee: Cost-Reduction Measures — recommended to full Council.
    Staff presented three categories of value-engineering measures totaling $58.1M in savings: (1) Construction methods and sequence — $14M (modified staging area, alternative pavement materials, reduced sewer relocation); (2) Design modifications — $4.8M (eliminating meandering pathway at Rengstorff Park, replacing high retaining walls with sloped embankments); (3) Construction duration reductions and indirect cost savings — $39.3M (shortening construction from 36 to 30 months, reducing contingency). These measures reduce the total estimate from $453M to $395M, with $296M in secured/anticipated funding and a remaining ~$99M gap. The Committee recommended approval to City Council.
    Legistar #205379
  • November 18, 2025 ↗
    City Council: Cost-Reduction Measures approved — Project 22-27.
    Council approved the $58.1M cost-reduction package recommended by the Transportation Committee, reducing the project estimate from $453M to ~$395M. The remaining ~$99M funding gap is unresolved; staff was directed to pursue additional federal and state grant opportunities. Approximately 6% of total project costs (preliminary engineering through construction) are funded by the Shoreline Regional Park Community Fund. Caltrain remains lead agency for design and construction; the project is progressing through 65% design. No construction start date has been established.
    Legistar #205555

About this project

The Rengstorff Avenue Grade Separation (Project 22-27) will eliminate the at-grade Caltrain crossing at Rengstorff Avenue by depressing Rengstorff Avenue and Central Expressway below grade. Crisanto Avenue will terminate in a cul-de-sac; Leland Avenue will be reconnected further south with a signal. A bicycle/pedestrian bridge will span Rengstorff Avenue parallel to the rail tracks. Caltrain is the lead design/construction agency; VTA provides Measure B funding; the City of Mountain View is project sponsor. A separate project, the Mountain View Transit Center Grade Separation (Castro/Evelyn interim improvements), is not covered here.
